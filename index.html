I am someone who has always been a self-starter, and I believe my career up to this point demonstrates my tenacity. My parents didn't have the chance to go to college and the counselors at my school were unfortunately overworked and undertrained, to where they didn't even know what SAT subject tests were and how essential they were for college applications at the time. So, I navigated the college application process by myself.


Ultimately, I managed to complete two associate's degrees before I even graduated high school, where I then graduated as Salutatorian of our large school. I continued on to receive an Ivy League degree in Biology and Chemistry, and I am currently completing my second Ivy League degree: a Master's in Computer and Information Technology.


Because my interests lied in the biological sciences, I entered the field of clinical research. I now have four years of experience working in this area, from creating studies from scratch and seeing them through to their conclusion, to coming on to previously established studies and coordinating between its patients and different stakeholders to make sure it runs smoothly. Throughout my years of experience, I have had the pleasure to work with incredible individuals, improving my communication and leadership skills. At my last job, I trained new employees, supervised nurses during our shifts, managed the schedules of our study patients, and served as the liaison for the 21 staff working on the study, championing the art of a good email and facilitating conversations.
